To Begin:Including Peers

Students can sometimes better comprehend a comcept when they are allowed to explain their understanding to other students and then provide feedback to each other. This is peer learning. Such peer based interactions often occur beyond the classroom walls. Student-to-student explanations or reviews can be included using online tools. Peer learning can become peer assessment. And when peer assessments are written down, they can be rated and discussed.

Peer assessment works differently from teacher driven assessment. Perhaps because it opens the possibility for conversations about what is acceptable evidence of accomplishment. If students are also allowed to respond to or mark peers’ work, and exercise their own judgement with their peers’ work, then a better understanding of the process of assessment can emerge.
